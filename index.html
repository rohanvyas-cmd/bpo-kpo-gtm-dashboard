Executive Summary
TL;DR

India's pure-play BPM sector is a ~$54.6B FY25 export-led industry employing ~1.6M people, built on exactly the high-volume, rule-based, voice-and-document work that Nurix's three products automate — making India BPOs both the biggest opportunity and the most motivated buyers as AI threatens their labor-arbitrage economics.

The clearest Nurix fit is where work is phone-heavy, rules-based, offshore, and English-language: Healthcare RCM (eligibility / prior-auth / AR follow-up + claims docs), Collections / AR calling (BFSI), Insurance claims & policy admin, Mortgage processing, and CX inbound/outbound voice.

Recommended priority order: (1) Healthcare RCM → (2) Collections & AR → (3) CX voice → (4) Insurance ops → (5) Mortgage → (6) Banking KYC/AML → (7) F&A. Lead with payer-call automation — ROI math (cost-per-call, hold-time elimination, 24/7 throughput) is unambiguous and already proven by rivals.

Caveats & Data Notes
⚠ Market-size figures vary widely. India BPO $49.87B (Astute Analytica) vs. NASSCOM BPM $54.6B FY25 / ~$59B FY26; healthcare BPO global estimates range from ~$260B to ~$466B depending on scope. Treat all figures as directional, not precise.
⚠ Vendor ROI claims are unverified. "99% accuracy," "67% cost savings," "80% automation," "+15% denial recovery" originate from competing AI vendors' own marketing (Prosper, Infinitus, SuperDial, Vodex). ⚠ Regulatory accountability does not transfer. In KYC/AML (RBI/SEBI/IRDAI) and HIPAA contexts, the regulated entity keeps accountability. Automation must preserve audit trails and human oversight. Frame this as a core selling point — not a constraint to work around.
